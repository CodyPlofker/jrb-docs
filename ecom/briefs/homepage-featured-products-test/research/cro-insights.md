## Executive Summary

- **The featured products grid is the single most-looked-at element on the homepage** — 63% of Elder-Millennial prospects scan featured products first, and visuals dominate attention (Homepage Evaluation, ListenLabs, Jun 2025). What we put in the grid is load-bearing for first-session conversion intent.
- **The #1 customer-requested fix to the homepage is a clear "New Arrivals" section** — ranked #1 in homepage priority (avg rank 1.7, 49 of 91 first-place picks), and 49.5% say it is MISSING today (Website Feedback Typeform, n=91, Mar 2026). This is the most quantified homepage friction point we have.
- **Scanning leads with price + ratings** — prospects look for star ratings and price "early" on featured tiles (Homepage Evaluation Jun 2025); zero-review products cause a "glaring zero" drop-off. Review count, rating, and price visibility on tiles are likely higher-leverage than swapping which products appear.

## Current State Data

### Scanning Behavior
- **63% of Elder Millennial prospects scan featured products first** (Homepage Evaluation, ListenLabs, Jun 2025) — featured products win first attention over hero banner, press logos, quiz entry, and founder blocks.
- Only 17% notice "clean beauty" as the primary hook on first scan — visuals dominate, copy is secondary (Homepage Eval Jun 2025).
- Prospects look for **ratings and price early** on tiles; zero-review products cause drop-off ("glaring zero" problem).
- **Quiz is NOT a homepage draw**: 0.0% first-action pick, ranked 6th of 8 (Website Feedback Mar 2026). Don't replace featured grid with a quiz entry.

### What Should Be Featured (Rank Order of Priority)
From Website Feedback (n=91, Mar 2026):

**Cross-cut signals for kits**:

- 50.9% bought Bobbi Kit 5.0; "Bobbi's personal curation" = trust signal for bundles
- Executives (22% of revenue) over-index on Pinky Bronze Kit (112), Hero Kit (104)

### Mobile Behavior
- **53.8% shop on phone** (Website Feedback Mar 2026)
- Current homepage stacks 4 products vertically on mobile — means the 4th tile is deep below fold on most devices
- Mobile image friction confirmed: "when I try to zoom in it jumps to another photo" (verbatim, Website Feedback)
- No specific homepage scroll-depth data in `customer-intelligence.md` — **Heatmap.com MCP pull recommended** for site ID 2821 to quantify below-fold drop-off on the featured grid, CTR by tile position, and mobile-vs-desktop engagement delta before finalizing the variation.

### Social Proof Signals on Tiles
- Featured product tiles today do not consistently surface star ratings and review counts on the homepage (to be re-verified visually in the mockup phase).
- 39% of PDP prospects cite reviews as #1 trust builder; a 76K review count was explicitly called out as a "trust moment" (PDP Purchase Confidence Mar 2026).
- 273 Junip reviews use the phrase "holy grail" (Junip Intelligence Mar 2026) — a testable badge/micro-copy.
- "I don't care if you have five stars and you got two reviews, that doesn't mean anything" — age 55 verbatim — **volume matters as much as rating**.

## Top Hypotheses (with ICE Scores & Primary Metric)

ICE scale: Impact/Confidence/Ease each 1–10. Ease: higher = easier to ship.

### H1 — Add "New Arrivals" as the first featured row
**Hypothesis**: If we replace the current generic featured row with a clearly-labeled "New Arrivals" section (2–4 newest SKUs, "NEW" badge, real photography, star rating + review count on tile), then homepage → PDP click-through and add-to-cart rate will increase, because 49.5% of customers say this is missing and it's ranked #1 priority (n=91, Mar 2026).
- **Variation concept**: First row below hero = "Just In" / "New Arrivals" with 2 products on mobile / 4 on desktop, NEW badge, price, stars + count.
- **Primary metric**: Homepage → PDP CTR on featured tiles. **Secondary**: Homepage → ATC rate, revenue per session.
- **ICE**: I 9 · C 9 · E 8 = **26**

### H2 — Surface star rating + review count on every featured tile
**Hypothesis**: If we add star rating + review count (e.g., "4.8 ★ · 2,341 reviews") to every homepage featured tile, then homepage → PDP CTR will increase on products with established review volume, because prospects scan for ratings and price early (63% scan-first) and review volume is the #1 trust builder (39%, PDP study Mar 2026).
- **Variation concept**: Compact rating row (stars + integer count) directly under product name. Hide on products with <25 reviews to avoid "glaring zero" (Homepage Eval Jun 2025).
- **Primary metric**: Tile CTR, weighted by product review count. **Secondary**: Homepage bounce rate, session-to-PDP rate.
- **ICE**: I 8 · C 9 · E 8 = **25** (design-system star pattern already exists on PDP/collection pages — reuse verbatim per global rule)

### H3 — Persona-matched featured row: Kits for Executive/Matriarch, Singles for Prospect
**Hypothesis**: If we add a second featured row dedicated to curated kits/bundles ("Shop the Routines" / Bobbi-curated), then AOV and revenue per session will increase, because curated kits are preferred 35.2% vs 20.4% individual (Holiday Kit n=163) and Executives (22% of revenue, AOV $114) over-index on kits.
- **Variation concept**: Two rows — Row 1: "New Arrivals" (prospect/discovery intent); Row 2: "Bobbi's Curated Routines" with 3 kits (Hero Kit, Pinky Bronze, Bobbi Kit 5.0 style).
- **Primary metric**: AOV per homepage-entry session. **Secondary**: Kit SKU units per session, revenue per session.
- **ICE**: I 8 · C 7 · E 6 = **21**

### H4 — Mobile-first horizontal scroll for featured tiles
**Hypothesis**: If we change mobile featured products from 4-stacked-vertical to a 2-up horizontal scroll (swipeable, manual — NEVER auto-advance per design system), then below-fold tile CTR will increase, because 53.8% shop on mobile and the current stack buries tiles 3 and 4.
- **Variation concept**: 2 products visible per viewport on mobile, swipe to reveal 4–6 more; dot indicators; no auto-rotate.
- **Primary metric**: CTR on tiles in positions 3+, mobile only. **Secondary**: Mobile session → ATC, mobile bounce rate.
- **ICE**: I 7 · C 6 · E 5 = **18** (needs Heatmap.com scroll data to confirm drop-off hypothesis before committing)

### H5 — "Holy Grail" / "Bestseller" social-proof badges on tiles
**Hypothesis**: If we add a single qualifier badge to the top-1 bestseller ("Bestseller", "Holy Grail — 273 reviews use this phrase", "76K+ reviews"), then tile CTR and brand trust perception will increase, because review volume is a conversion trigger (39% #1 trust builder) and "holy grail" is a 273-mention natural-language pattern in existing Junip data.
- **Variation concept**: One badge per row, max; reuse `/jrb-section-library` badge pattern. No emojis.
- **Primary metric**: Tile CTR on badged products vs unbadged control; **Secondary**: Homepage ATC rate.
- **ICE**: I 6 · C 7 · E 9 = **22** (trivial to ship — copy/design only)

**Recommended starter stack**: H1 (New Arrivals row) + H2 (ratings on tiles) as the highest-ICE, lowest-conflict combined variation — test vs current control. H3 as the v2 once v1 is decisioned. H4 and H5 held as follow-on iterations pending Heatmap data and JRB01 results review.

## Test Design — Incremental Isolation (Apr 15, 2026 update)

**Context**: An earlier draft of this brief bundled 4 hypotheses into each arm (e.g., V1 = rename + stars + badge + 2×2 grid). Cody pushed back: if V1 wins, we can't attribute the lift to any single lever. Replaced with an incremental-isolation design so each arm differs from the prior arm by exactly one variable.

**The four arms**:

| Arm | Layers on top of... | Isolates | What we learn if it wins |
|-----|--------------------|---------|----------------------------|
| V1 Control | — | (baseline) | Today's ceiling on PDP CTR with no stars/reviews/price |
| V2 +Social proof | V1 | Stars + honest review counts + price + optional NEW badge | Social proof alone is enough — no layout change needed |
| V3 +Carousel | V2 | Layout (carousel vs 4-up grid, 6 products in same vertical space) | Layout is the binding constraint — mobile scroll reduction drives the lift |
| V4 +Multi-section | V3 | Curation variety (Bestsellers / New / Bobbi's Picks shelves) | Multiple curation lenses beat a single "Featured" row → Phase 2 A/B of curation types on V3 carousel |

**Why this beats a bundled design**:
1. Clean attribution per lever — no "V1 won but we don't know why" debate.
2. V4's row-level click share is a <strong>free diagnostic</strong> for Phase 2 — tells us which curation lens to put into a clean A/B (Bestsellers-only vs New-only vs Bobbi's-only) without running an additional 5-arm curation test first.
3. Deferred low-confidence hypotheses (customer-phrase badges, category toggle, inline ATC) keeps each arm interpretable and the dev scope tight.

**Honest review counts over hidden counts**: Original mockups proposed hiding review counts under 100 to avoid the "glaring zero" problem. Cody corrected this: show the real number (e.g., Blushing Stick = 26 reviews) and add an optional NEW badge for just-launched SKUs. The NEW badge reframes low counts as "this just dropped" rather than "unproven." Preserves honesty; handles the newness-context problem.

**What's held for Phase 2+**:
- Clean A/B of curation <em>type</em> on the winning layout (informed by V4 row-level share)
- Customer-phrase badges ("Holy Grail", "Life-changing") — deferred so they don't confound the V2 social-proof isolation
- Inline ATC on tile (requires variant-selection UX — scope creep)
- Category toggle — survey rank #6, not worth an arm
