5. The Four Test Cells — Incremental Isolation

Each variation layers one change on top of the last. That way if V3 beats V2, we know the lift came from layout — not a confound. If V4 beats V3, we know the lift came from curation variety. Clean attribution per lever, no more "V1 won but we don't know why."

V1 — Control
Current "Featured Products" grid
Baseline

What ships today

4 products in a horizontal grid on desktop. No ratings, no review counts, no badges. Mobile stacks all four tiles vertically — creates ~2,400px of scroll before the next section.

Why it matters as control

This is the surface 63% of homepage visitors engage with organically (Website Feedback, n=91). Any lift here compounds across ~283K sessions/month.
V2 — + Social proof
Control + stars, review counts, price, optional NEW badge
Isolates social proof
Open full mockup →

Hypothesis

Adding Junip stars, honest review counts, and price on every tile raises PDP CTR 8–14% by giving scanners the social-proof signal they're already looking for. Layout is unchanged from control — any lift is attributable to social proof alone.

Variable

  • 5-star rating (partial-fill, e.g. 4.23☆)
  • Honest review count (e.g. "84,634 reviews" on Miracle Balm, "26 reviews" on Blushing Stick)
  • Price visible on every card
  • Optional NEW badge for just-launched SKUs — contextualizes low counts without hiding them

Risk

Low review counts on new products (Blushing Stick, 26) could read as weak. Mitigated by the NEW badge, which reframes "26 reviews" as "this just dropped" rather than "this is unproven."
V3 — + Carousel
V2 + horizontal swipe carousel (6 products in same vertical space)
Isolates layout
Open full mockup →

Hypothesis

Swapping the 4-up grid for a swipeable carousel cuts mobile scroll ~70% vs. control and surfaces 6 products instead of 4. Social proof stays identical to V2 — any delta vs. V2 is attributable to layout alone.

Variable

  • 4-up grid → horizontal carousel (desktop: 4 visible, swipe for 5–6)
  • Mobile: 1.2 tiles visible, swipe for the rest (~460px scroll vs ~2,400px)
  • Product count 4 → 6 (adds Mini Miracle Balm, Just Enough Tinted Moisturizer)
  • Manual swipe only, no auto-advance (per brand guidelines)

Risk

Tiles 5–6 get less exposure than a grid would give. Progress dots + "Swipe to see more" hint mitigate; also gives us carousel-engagement data (swipe rate) as a secondary signal.
V4 — + Multi-section
V3 + three curated shelves: Bestsellers / New Arrivals / Bobbi's Picks
Isolates curation
Open full mockup →

Hypothesis

Three curated carousel shelves (instead of one generic "Featured" row) address multiple shopper mindsets simultaneously — proven bestsellers for skeptics, newness for repeat customers, founder curation for trust-driven prospects. Expected +10–18% total PDP CTR vs. V3 via increased product surface area and curation relevance.

Variable

  • Bestsellers shelf — WTF, Mini Miracle Balm, Lip Recharge, Miracle Balm
  • New Arrivals shelf (alt bg) — Blushing Stick (NEW), Eyeshadow Stick (NEW), Just Enough TM, Lip Recharge
  • Bobbi's Picks shelf — Miracle Balm, WTF, Just Enough TM, Lip Recharge
  • Alternating white / gray backgrounds to visually separate rows

Why this unlocks Phase 2

Row-level click share tells us which curation lens customers prefer. If Bestsellers takes 60%+ of clicks, Phase 2 is a clean A/B of Bestsellers-only vs. New-only vs. Bobbi's-only on the V3 carousel layout — no more bundling.

Risk

Three shelves extends the homepage vertically. Counter: each shelf is a single-row carousel (not stacked grids), so the total added height is ~2.2× a single section, not 3×.

6. Metrics, Tracking, Sample Size

Primary metric

Tile-to-PDP click-through rate — homepage sessions that click any featured product tile, divided by homepage sessions that scrolled past the section's top edge. Measured in GA4 via homepage_featured_tile_click event + section_impression event (fires when element enters viewport ≥ 50%).

Events to fire

All events include variation_id (v1 / v2 / v3 / v4), tile_position, tile_product_handle, device_class, and (V4 only) shelf_id (bestsellers / new / bobbis_picks).

Sample size & duration

At 283K monthly homepage sessions entering this section and a baseline tile CTR of ~11.4% (tile 1+2+3 combined), an 8% MDE at 4 cells, 80% power, 95% confidence requires ~12 days. Plan: run 14 days for weekday/weekend coverage. 25% traffic allocation per cell.

Pre-flight guard Because JRB01 already touched this surface (Dec 2025) and its hover-state tweak was the last structural change here, pair this test with a week-over-week regression check on tile CTR for the 7 days preceding launch. If baseline drifts >8%, hold the launch and re-sample.
