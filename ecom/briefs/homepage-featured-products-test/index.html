Primary Hypothesis
If we replace the generic 4-up "Featured Products" grid with a data-driven merchandising pattern — surfacing New Arrivals (the #1 missing signal), adding star ratings on every tile (the #1 scanning behavior), and shrinking mobile scroll — we expect a 10–18% lift in tile-to-PDP click-through and a 4–8% lift in homepage-attributed add-to-cart rate, with directional AOV gains in the kit variant.

The synthesis Featured Products is the most-engaged organic module on the homepage, yet it ranks as the #1 missing signal in customer feedback ("no new arrivals" is the most common complaint). On mobile — two thirds of sessions — the current layout stacks all four tiles vertically, creating ~2,400px of scroll. There is no rating, no badge, no urgency signal on any tile. Every competitor we audited surfaces at least two of those three.

2. Customer Evidence

Four verbatim signals behind the four variations. Each is tagged to the variation it informs.

Evidence A — "New Arrivals" is the #1 missing thing V1 · V2

Signal: 49.5% of Website Feedback respondents (n=91) rank "New Arrivals" as a missing homepage element, making it the highest-ranked gap. Average rank in the "what would you add?" question: 1.7 of 5 options.

"I come back to see what's new. I don't want to scroll past the same four balms I already own."
"When is the new thing coming out? Just tell me on the homepage."

Source: Website Feedback Typeform · Q3 "What would you add to the homepage?" · Jan–Apr 2026

Evidence B — Ratings are scanned before copy V1 · V3 · V4

Signal: 63% of Listenlabs prospect interviewees describe scanning product cards for star ratings and review counts before reading product names or prices. Price is read second (71% cite price anxiety). Product names are read last.

"I look for the little stars first. If there's no stars I just skip it."
"How many reviews? Is it like thousands or is it like twelve? That tells me everything."

Source: Listenlabs Website Eval study · Prospect browse think-alouds · n=28 · March 2026

Evidence C — Kits and routines drive Executive AOV V2

Signal: Executive persona (ages 45–54, 38% of repeat customers by OuterSignal) has an AOV of $114 vs. $82 brand average. 61% of her orders contain a multi-product bundle (kit, duo, or trio). Kits today are buried in the main nav — not shown on the homepage below hero.

"I don't have time to figure out which six things to buy. Just give me the set Bobbi would pick."

Source: OuterSignal persona overlay (Executive) · Shopify AOV report · JRB customer intelligence March 2026

Evidence D — "Holy Grail" is the dominant organic descriptor V4

Signal: Across 24,108 Junip reviews for Miracle Balm, "holy grail" appears 273 times — the single most-used organic phrase that does not appear in any brand-authored copy. "Life changing" (91) and "game changer" (64) are next. No product in the JRB catalog uses any of this language on the tile, PDP, or hero.

"Bought this on a whim and now it's a holy grail product for me."

Source: Junip review text mining · Miracle Balm SKU · all-time · pulled April 12, 2026

Each cell maps to a different combination of the top six hypotheses. V1 is the single-hypothesis highest-ICE cell; V2–V4 pair levers.

Control — Baseline
Current "Featured Products" grid

What ships today

4 products in a horizontal grid on desktop. No ratings, no badges, no shade counts. Mobile stacks all four tiles vertically — creates ~2,400px of scroll before the next section.

Why it matters as control

This is the surface 63% of homepage visitors engage with organically. Any lift here compounds across ~283K sessions/month.
Variation 01 — Highest ICE (26 + 25)
New Arrivals row + ratings on every tile
ICE 26 + 25
Open full mockup →

Hypothesis

If we rename "Featured products" → "New arrivals", add a "New · This Week" badge to the two most-recent SKUs, and surface star rating + review count on every tile, tile-to-PDP CTR will lift 10–15%.

Levers

  • Content (rename + eyebrow)
  • Social proof (stars + counts)
  • Urgency (NEW · This Week)
  • Mobile UX (2×2 grid, halves scroll)

Risk

Star ratings may visually compress the tile — tested at 11px (compact Junip icon) to stay within the tile's existing height budget.

Hypothesis

A tab bar lets visitors self-sort (ILIA's "Curated Favorites" pattern), addressing the 42% "I don't know where to start" signal. Expected lift: +6–12% CTR on mid-funnel prospects; neutral/negative on return customers who already know what they want.

Variation 04 — Mobile UX + Language (ICE 18 + 22)
Mobile carousel + Holy Grail badge
ICE 18 + 22
Open full mockup →

Hypothesis

(A) Replacing the mobile 4-stack with a 1.2-visible horizontal carousel reduces scroll from ~2,400px to ~460px, keeping subsequent homepage sections within fold reach for a materially larger share of mobile sessions. (B) Pink "Holy Grail · 273 reviews" badge on Miracle Balm surfaces the dominant organic review phrase, which does not appear anywhere in current site copy.

Levers

  • Mobile UX (carousel replaces stack)
  • Language (customer phrase on badge)
  • Social proof (stars + counts)
  • Scroll efficiency (80% reduction vs. control)

Risk

Carousel requires manual swipe; tiles 3–4 get less exposure. Counter: progress dots + "Swipe to see more" hint text; manual control only (no auto-advance, per brand guidelines).

6. Metrics, Tracking, Sample Size

Primary metric

Tile-to-PDP click-through rate — homepage sessions that click any featured product tile, divided by homepage sessions that scrolled past the section's top edge. Measured in GA4 via homepage_featured_tile_click event + section_impression event (fires when element enters viewport ≥ 50%).

Events to fire

All events include variation_id (control / v1 / v2 / v3 / v4), tile_position, tile_product_handle, and device_class.

Sample size & duration

At 283K monthly homepage sessions entering this section and a baseline tile CTR of ~11.4% (tile 1+2+3 combined), a 10% MDE at 5 cells, 80% power, 95% confidence requires ~11 days. Plan: run 14 days for weekday/weekend coverage. 20% traffic allocation per cell.

Pre-flight guard Because JRB01 already touched this surface (Dec 2025) and its hover-state tweak was the last structural change here, pair this test with a week-over-week regression check on tile CTR for the 7 days preceding launch. If baseline drifts >8%, hold the launch and re-sample.
