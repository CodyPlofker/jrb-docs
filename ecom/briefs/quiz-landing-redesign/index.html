01

Problem statement

The current quiz landing page (/pages/quiz-landing) underperforms across every funnel metric. The quiz selector -- the page's core value prop -- is buried below a full-viewport video hero and a 6-quiz grid that dilutes intent.

  • Traffic: 11.8K desktop / 18.5K mobile sessions, 976 conversions (3/3 - 3/15/2026)
  • Hero waste: Video hero takes the full viewport -- quiz options are buried below the fold
  • Scroll drop-off: 40-50% of visitors leave before reaching the quiz grid
  • Quiz grid bloat: 6 quizzes, but Row 3 (Bronzer, Tinted Powder) gets roughly 30% visibility
  • Dead content: "Still need help?" section has near-zero engagement

The quiz selector IS the page's value prop, but it is treated as secondary content.

02

Research & inspiration

  • AG1 stepped funnel: The action IS the page. No wasted hero, no passive content above the primary CTA.
  • Merit Beauty shade quiz: Clean "FIND MY SHADE" as the primary conversion path -- single-purpose hero.
  • Heatmap insight: Row 1 quizzes are the hottest zones. Simplifying to 2 paths captures the majority of intent.
  • Bobbi Brown credibility: Founder authority serves as a powerful trust anchor further down the page.
03

Strategic direction

  • 6 quizzes down to 2 paths: Miracle Balm (live) + Consolidated Complexion Quiz (in development)
  • Passive directory becomes active funnel: The page pre-sells the quiz, not a product grid
  • Quiz selector above the fold on all devices -- desktop and mobile
  • Full page becomes a "pre-sell" experience: Every section below the hero exists to build confidence and drive quiz starts
04

Two variations

V2-A: Flipped Hero

Layout

Desktop: Quiz cards LEFT, model image RIGHT.
Mobile: Image hidden, quiz cards first.

Rationale

Western left-to-right reading pattern places quiz cards in the primary scan position. The model image provides emotional warmth on desktop without competing for attention.

Trade-off

Loses the emotional hook of the model image on mobile. Returning visitors who already trust the brand will benefit most from this layout.

Best for

Returning visitors who already trust the brand.

V2-B: No Hero Image

Layout

Desktop & mobile: Centered quiz selector, no image. Maximum above-the-fold conversion real estate.

Rationale

AG1 pattern -- the best-converting pages have zero hero imagery. Every pixel above the fold drives quiz starts.

Trade-off

Less aspirational. May feel "utility" vs "brand." Mitigated by the qualification section, Bobbi credibility, and testimonials providing warmth below the fold.

Best for

Fastest path to quiz start -- pure conversion play.

Comparison

Metric V2-A Flipped V2-B No Image

09

Recommendation

Test both variations

We recommend testing both V2-A and V2-B against the current page as an A/B test. V2-A is the safer bet -- it retains brand imagery on desktop and provides an emotional anchor for first-time visitors. V2-B is the bolder play -- maximum conversion focus with zero visual distractions above the fold. Both solve the core problem: quiz cards above the fold on every device.
