The Problem

Miracle Balm is JRB's highest-traffic PDP and the entry point for most new customers. But the current page is losing conversions — not because customers can't find products, but because they can't confidently choose.

Five independent research streams converge on the same conclusion: the PDP fails to educate, fails to build shade confidence, and fails to communicate the product's extraordinary value.

The Acquisition Gap

Existing customer research captures people who already bought. Prospect data reveals problems invisible to existing customers: product comprehension failure, price-as-risk (not price-as-expensive), and shade quiz distrust.

Key Insights

1. Product Comprehension Failure

7 out of 23 prospects thought Miracle Balm was a lip balm. The multi-use positioning creates skepticism rather than excitement among people who haven't tried it. We need to lead with ONE clear use case (cream blush) and reveal versatility second.

"It says balm, which makes me think it's immediately all about the lips. When I look, there's videos... at least two of them are using it on their cheeks."— ListenLabs Participant 0033, age 44
7/23 Prospects 1,301 "how to use" mentions

2. Shade Confidence Is the #1 Barrier

Confirmed by ALL five data sources. Average shade confidence is just 3.58 out of 5. 76.3% of Typeform respondents want swatches on 3+ skin tones — it's the single most-requested PDP addition. The shade quiz is actively distrusted by prospects.

3. Price = Risk, Not "Expensive"

Prospects spend $50+ on proven products but won't spend $38 on an untried one. The mini is essential for acquisition — 9/23 prospects explicitly asked for a sample. Meanwhile, JRB's $21.59/oz value (vs $49–$165/oz competitors) is completely invisible on the PDP.

"I would not pay $38 on a brand new product that I've never tested before. I did go to Ulta last week and bought my foundation... it's like $50 — but it's tested."— ListenLabs Participant 0033, age 44

4. Bobbi Is the Most Underutilized Asset

55.1% of customers rate Bobbi's importance as 5 out of 5 (maximum). Reviews mentioning Bobbi average 4.34–4.78 stars vs overall averages. Yet Bobbi is barely present on the PDP — her tips are buried in an accordion, her videos live on the blog.

5. "Products Model Is Wearing" — The Surprise #2 Gap

53.9% of Typeform respondents say "a list of products the model is wearing" is missing from PDPs — the second-most requested addition after shade swatches. This is both a shade confidence tool and a cross-sell opportunity.

6. Reviews Are Critical But Mismanaged

76K+ reviews is JRB's biggest competitive moat (MERIT has 6.9K, Rare Beauty 4.9K). But reviews showing zero kills conversion — moving reviews below the fold hurt conversions. Review count and ratings should be prominent near the ATC.

Policy Decision Needed

Shade Confidence Guarantee

MERIT has a 30-Day Shade Match Promise. We recommend an equivalent: "Not your shade? We'll help you find the right one — free exchanges, free returns, no questions asked." This requires a policy decision from CS/Ops team before the section can go live.

Study to Commission

Consumer Perception Study — $15-30K

ILIA shows "100% said moisture was boosted" on PDP. JRB has zero efficacy claims. A consumer perception study would provide credibility data for PDP, ads, and PR. Section 13 shows what the format would look like.
