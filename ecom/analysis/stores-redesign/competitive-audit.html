Stores Page Audit

March 27, 2026 8 brands audited 4 skipped (inaccessible)
Key Insight
At 12 stores, you don't need a map. You need a gallery.
Brands with fewer than 15 stores (Everlane, Away) go editorial and visual. Brands with 100+ stores (Warby Parker, Apple) go utility and map-driven. JRB at 12 stores today, growing to 24, should start editorial and add search later.

Universal Patterns (table stakes)
01
Some form of store finding
Search by location, map pins, or a browsable list. Every brand offers at least one.
02
Individual store detail pages
Clicking a store links to a page with hours, address, directions, sometimes booking.
03
Location-based naming
Stores are named by neighborhood/city, not numbers. "Prince Street, New York" not "Store #47".
04
Open/closed status + directions
Real-time availability and a direct link to Google Maps directions.
Common Patterns (worth considering)
01
Visual store grid with photography
Unique store photos per location. Treats each store as a visual destination, not just a pin on a map.

Whitespace for JRB (things nobody does)
Founder Connection
Nobody ties the founder to the retail experience. JRB has Bobbi Brown as a massive asset. "Get color-matched the Bobbi way."
Book a Consultation
Only Warby Parker and Apple offer booking. For a beauty brand where shade matching matters, this is a massive opportunity.
"Opening Soon" Teaser
With JRB going 12 to 24 stores, showcase upcoming locations. Build anticipation and capture email signups.
Per-Store Products
No brand shows which products are popular at each location. "Try these at Montclair" with shade matching or bestsellers.
Store-Specific Social Proof
No brand shows reviews or UGC from specific store locations. Surface location-specific testimonials.
In-Store Experiences
Market what you'll DO there — free makeovers, skin consultations, shade matching, Bobbi masterclasses.
