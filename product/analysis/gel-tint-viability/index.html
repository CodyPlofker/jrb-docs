Gel Tint Is the Kit Star

When customers call out individual products in their kit reviews, the Gel Tint is overwhelmingly the standout. Multiple reviewers explicitly ask JRB to sell it as a permanent standalone product. Meanwhile, the Lippie Stick is the most-criticized component (described as dry and hard to apply).

Packaging Fix Required Before Scaling

The tube packaging is the #1 complaint across Gel Tint mentions. Customers report the product oozing out of the tube, making a mess, and wasting product. This is a solvable packaging problem, not a formula problem. Fixing this before standalone launch is critical to maintaining the strong 4.36 rating at higher volume.

Next Steps

  1. Fix the tube packaging before standalone launch. Customers love the formula but the tube oozes and leaks, which will drive negative reviews at higher volume.
  2. Confirm March sales cliff is inventory-driven (check Shopify inventory logs for OOS dates).
  3. Launch Gel Tint standalone in Dusty Rose first (2.5x stronger demand), Sunkissed as fast follow.
  4. Evaluate standalone pricing: kits were ~$59 AOV. Standalone Gel Tint could unlock a lower entry price point and drive trial.
  5. Address color payoff for darker skin tones — expand shade range or adjust formula for deeper shades.
  6. Keep kit offerings alongside standalone to preserve the bundle value play and higher AOV.
