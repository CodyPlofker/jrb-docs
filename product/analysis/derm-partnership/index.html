Section 04

Deal Structure & Market Intel

What partnerships actually cost, what Neutrogena just paid, and how the Bhanusali-Rhode model worked. This is the intel that shapes negotiation.

The Bhanusali-Rhode Model (Gold Standard)

Dr. Dhaval Bhanusali was involved from day one — not an afterthought advisory role. He led formulation, ingredient selection, and used clinical skin analysis tools. Rhode generated $212M in revenue in the 12 months ending March 2025, doubled its customer base YoY, and was acquired by e.l.f. for $1 billion. Elle dubbed him the "King of Formulation." His visibility is behind-the-scenes (trade press, his own social channels) while Hailey is the consumer face.

This is the closest analog to what JRB should do — Bobbi is the face, a credible derm provides the science backbone.

Neutrogena Validates the Strategy

In October 2024, Kenvue announced multi-year "Global Innovation Partner" deals with Dr. Bhanusali and Dr. Shah. The biggest skincare brand in the world decided it needed specific, named dermatologists with social followings — not anonymous "derm recommended." Three focus areas: R&D partnership, consumer community building, and derm ecosystem development. If Neutrogena needs named derm partners to stay competitive, JRB certainly does.

Section 05

What This Enables

A derm with reach isn't just credibility — it's a distribution channel. It unlocks five concrete capabilities JRB doesn't have today.

1
Proprietary Ingredient Framework
"Bobbi's Skin Prep Complex" co-developed with Dr. [X]. Tatcha has Hadasei-3, Tula has S6Pro, Charlotte Tilbury has Recoverstem. JRB currently has nothing.
2
Clinical Claims
"Clinically shown to improve skin texture in 4 weeks" backed by real testing. Closes the credibility gap between JRB and clinical-first competitors.
3
Infinite Content Engine
Bobbi + derm conversations about "what actually works." YouTube, Instagram, email, podcast. The skeptic + the scientist is an endlessly compelling format.
4
Move the "Worth Paying More For" Needle
Tracksuit shows JRB at 38% vs. 44% category average on "worth paying more for." A derm co-sign adds perceived value without raising prices.
5
Own "Expert Skin Prep" with Clinical Teeth
Without a derm, it's a marketing claim. With a derm, it's clinically validated positioning. The difference between saying it and proving it.

Section 07

Next Steps

Five steps to move from research to first conversations. The window is closing — every month, another derm announces their own line.

Step 1
Internal Alignment
Present concept to Bobbi and leadership. Key question: do we want a derm who brings reach (Zubritsky at 3.6M) or one who brings philosophical fit (Engelman at 100K but mirrors JRB's ethos)? The answer shapes who to call first.
Step 2
Outreach to Tier 1
Exploratory conversations with Dr. Zubritsky, Dr. Sugai, Dr. Joyce Park, and Dr. Engelman. For Zubritsky, confirm All Free Clear / Avene exclusivity terms. For Sugai, check Olay terms. Contact MN2S agency for any candidates they represent (Zeichner, Dray, others).
Step 3
Define the Role & Budget
Formalize title (Chief Skin Science Officer or similar), time commitment (5–15 hrs/month), compensation structure (retainer $200–500K/yr for 1M+ follower derm, or equity 3–5% for deeper partnership), exclusivity terms, and content expectations.
Step 4
Test with CAB
Add 2–3 derm credibility questions to the next Customer Advisory Board survey. Validate that "dermatologist-developed" moves the needle for JRB's core consumer. Test with and without specific names/photos to measure the "famous derm" premium vs. generic derm claim.
Step 5
Chemistry Meetings
The most important variable is whether Bobbi and the derm have natural chemistry. Arrange casual meetings (not pitch meetings) between Bobbi and 2–3 finalists. The partnership only works if the Bobbi + derm dynamic feels authentic on camera.
