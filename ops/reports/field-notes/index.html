What matters

Two patterns and an adoption problem.

Two of the three entries land on the same theme: packaging that's hard for older customers to use. The third is a service idea from one store that could expand the floor's revenue mix.

01

Packaging is failing the 65+ customer.

Two entries, two cities (Bucktown, Dallas). Tinted Moisturizer caps explode in transit; Everyday Sunscreen tubes are too stiff for arthritic hands. One customer already churned to another sunscreen brand.

2 entries 2 stores High severity
So what JRB skews 65+ in the Tracksuit data. Cap and dispense ergonomics are not edge cases for this customer base — they're table stakes. This pattern needs corroboration but the consequence is real: lost repeats.
02

Stores want a shorter, lower-priced service tier.

Chestnut Hill is asking for a 20–30 min mini-makeup service at the $35 (Eyes Only) or $75 (Teens) price point — usable as event slots and as a launch-window vehicle for new product education.

1 entry 1 store Medium severity
So what Single source, but a coherent business idea: more bookable inventory, faster cycle, built-in product education moment. Pilotable in one store before scaling.
03

14 of 17 stores have submitted nothing.

Bucktown, Chestnut Hill, and Dallas are the only stores using Field Notes. Atlanta, Austin, Charleston, Cobble Hill, East Hampton, Greenwich Village, Montclair, Nashville, Palm Beach, Palo Alto, Santa Monica, Seaport, Upper East Side, and Williamsburg are silent.
