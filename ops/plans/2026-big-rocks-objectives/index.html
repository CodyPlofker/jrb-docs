2026 Big Rocks — Objectives Draft

The 6 Big Rocks and the objectives that bring them to life

Draft v2 March 2026 For review: Elizabeth
How to read this: Each Big Rock is a company-level strategic priority. The objectives underneath are the ambitious outcomes that, if accomplished, mean we've actually lived that Big Rock. Think of them as the "O" in OKRs. Output KPIs (measurable results) and Input KPIs (activities that drive results) will layer underneath each objective.
01 Become Customer Obsessed

Understand our customers deeply and use those insights to drive every decision we make.

Objectives
1. Establish a Customer Advisory Board and integrate it into key processes
Create a formal mechanism to hear from customers regularly — real customers, real feedback, on a consistent cadence. Embed the CAB into product development, launch planning, and marketing decisions so it’s not just a listening exercise.
2. Infuse customer obsession into every decision
Every product, calendar launch, promotion, marketing copy, and digital product decision starts with: what customer insights do we have? What’s our hypothesis? What new feedback do we need? Sources include surveys, studies, direct conversations, and retail team input.
3. Build a single source of truth for customer insights
Every team should be able to access the same research, surveys, retail feedback, and review data. No more silos.
4. Build and invest in customer community
Create real-life moments and programs that foster genuine community — events, IRL activations, and spaces where customers connect with each other and the brand. This is something we haven’t invested in before, and it’s time.
02 Become Creator Obsessed

Put creators at the center of everything we do. Understand how they think, create, and operate — and make JRB the brand every creator wants to work with.

Objectives
1. Modernize our social strategy with a creator-first approach
Shift from brand-produced to creator-driven content across channels. Creators aren't just a paid media lever — they're the voice.
2. Build a large, thriving creator community
Not just a roster of paid partners, but an actual community of creators who love the brand, talk to each other, and want to be part of JRB.
3. Scale creator efforts across both earned and paid
More creators, more content, more reach. Grow both organic/seeded creator content and paid partnerships.
4. Establish a Creator Advisory Panel
A small group of key creators who give direct input on product, packaging, content strategy, and launches. Mirrors the Customer Advisory Board from Big Rock 1.
03 Define Our Brand Strategy

Get clear on who we are beyond Bobbi — our mission, positioning, and what makes us unique and different. The lack of brand clarity is holding us back across every function.

Objectives
1. Define our brand positioning
Who are we, what makes us different, what do we stand for, where are we going. Build an identity that stands on its own — not just on Bobbi. Use customer insights, competitive analysis, and internal alignment to land on a positioning that gives every team a clear north star.
2. Embed the brand strategy across the entire organization
Everyone on the team should understand it, know it, feel it, and be able to run with it. Every decision — from product to marketing to retail — should be made with the brand strategy in mind. When that happens, we stop pulling in five different directions and start building toward the same thing.
3. Get Miracle Balm back on track
Dedicated leadership and strategy to re-establish Miracle Balm as our anchor product. This is both a product marketing and brand challenge.
4. Make the positioning the operating system, not a deck on a shelf
It should directly inform campaigns, messaging, partnership decisions, product development, retail experience, and creator briefs. If it doesn’t change how we work, it didn’t work.
04 Expand Distribution Channels

D2C growth is getting harder. We grew fast on DTC but haven't expanded quickly enough into other channels. Time to diversify aggressively.

Objectives
1. Aggressively scale owned retail
Stores perform phenomenally. Open new locations per the 2026 plan and make each one profitable and brand-building.
2. Launch and grow Amazon as a major channel
Big untapped opportunity. Get the strategy, operations, and catalog in place to make Amazon a real revenue driver.
3. Scale TikTok Shop
Already launched — now grow it into a meaningful revenue channel with creator affiliates and a full catalog.
4. Explore selective, small wholesale partnerships
No large wholesale deals, but be open to strategic smaller partnerships that align with the brand and expand reach.
5. Diversify revenue mix away from D2C dependence
The overall goal: build a multi-channel business where no single channel is a vulnerability.
05 Invest in Our Brand

We haven't really invested in our brand. Long-term growth depends on building brand equity, even when the return isn't immediate.

Objectives
1. Begin measuring brand health and use it to guide strategy
Tracksuit is running — now actually use the data. Track sentiment, awareness, consideration, and perception quarterly, and build strategies to improve the metrics that matter most.
2. Launch upper-funnel and brand marketing campaigns
Invest in reach campaigns, brand storytelling, and awareness plays that won't have immediate ROI but build long-term demand and brand equity.
3. Build an experiential and sampling program
In-person events, retail activations, sampling at scale. Get the product in people's hands and create real-life brand moments.
4. Grow social and creator programs as brand-building channels
Not just performance — reach, cultural relevance, and brand love. Overlaps with Big Rock 2 but the lens here is brand investment.
5. Shift the organizational mindset toward long-term brand investment
Build internal buy-in that not everything needs to show a return today. Some spend is about building the brand for where we want to be in 2–3 years.
06 Create a Culture of Greatness

Create an environment where greatness is the standard and people are proud of the work they do and the place they do it.

Objectives
1. Define a new set of core values and mission
Create values that actually mean something and give everyone a clear foundation for how we operate and make decisions.
2. Communicate and embed the values into how we work
Roll them out, train on them, and create consistency so they're not just words on a wall. Every process, review, and decision should ladder back to them.
3. Build a culture of feedback, accountability, and trust
Make giving and receiving feedback normal and safe. Not a top-down thing — a company-wide muscle we build together.
4. Create a high-performing, accountable environment
People own their outcomes, KPIs are real, standards are high, and everyone is proud of the work and the team.
