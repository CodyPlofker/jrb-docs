H1 Highest Confidence

Price and shipping shock at payment

IA's broader reach pulls in 33% more unique impressions at 25% lower CPM — by design, it targets less purchase-intent audiences. These users initiate checkout to see the final total (with shipping), then abandon when the number exceeds their mental budget.

Evidence from customer intelligence
  • 71% of JRB prospects cite price as a real barrier to purchase (ListenLabs, 11 studies)
  • Busy Suburban Super-Mom — highest abandonment persona (44.9% repeat rate, lowest loyalty) — is over-represented in broad-reach campaigns
  • IA CPM is $30.34 vs $40.46 for Control: cheaper inventory = broader, less committed audience
  • Mini/trial sizes absent: 20 of 21 respondents want lower-commitment entry point at $10–$15 price point — broad audience hits full AOV ($89) with no trial option
H2 High Confidence

Returns and trust deficit for new visitors

IA's wider audience contains a higher proportion of first-time JRB visitors who have not yet resolved brand trust. At the payment step, vague return language and lack of explicit guarantees block conversion for first-timers.

Evidence from customer intelligence
  • 22% of PDP study respondents say "free returns" is insufficient — they want explicit time limit and opened-product rules
  • JRB07 A/B test (n=1.16M): adding star reviews + "Free Returns Within 30 Days" to checkout generated +$0.08 RPV, +0.07% CVR
  • Brand-site review skepticism: prospects trust Ulta/Sephora more than brand-site reviews — new IA visitors haven't built brand trust yet
  • BAU Control's targeted audience has higher prior brand familiarity → less friction at payment step
H3 Medium Confidence

Shade and product confidence anxiety

IA drives more upper-funnel clicks from less brand-familiar users who have not yet resolved shade or product uncertainty. These users initiate checkout to "lock in" an item while still uncertain — then abandon to research further before committing.

Evidence from customer intelligence
  • Shade/undertone uncertainty is penta-confirmed #1 pre-purchase blocker: 46% of prospects, 26.9% of non-purchasers, 7,048 CX tickets/quarter
  • 50.5% of prospects score 1–3 on shade confidence; 78% want swatches on 3+ skin tones before buying
  • IA's higher Click-to-ATC% (16.7% vs 14.7%) suggests curiosity-driven adds-to-cart, not intent-driven — more users exploring before deciding
  • Product identity confusion: 10% initially confused Miracle Balm for lip balm; multi-use benefit requires scrolling to understand
H4 Medium Confidence

Attribution measurement artifact

IA's broader delivery may create more overlap with other channels (email, SMS, organic). When a user is served an IA ad, initiates checkout, then converts via a Klaviyo email, Meta records the checkout attribution but not the purchase — inflating the IC count relative to attributed purchases.

Evidence and logic
  • IA reaches 33% more unique impressions at lower CPM → greater overlap with retargeting and email audiences
  • IA's InitiateCheckout winning score is 87.7% (strong) — meaning IA genuinely drives more checkout intent — but Purchase winning score is only 62.5%
  • The gap between IC lift (87.7%) and Purchase lift (62.5%) is consistent with cross-channel attribution leakage
  • This is a measurement gap, not a real behavioral drop-off — true incrementality may be better than reported
Recommended actions to close the checkout gap
Strengthen checkout trust signals: replace "Free Returns" with "Free Returns Within 30 Days · Opened products accepted" + star review count (203K+ reviews) — directly tested in JRB07 (+$0.08 RPV)
Add non-gated shade finder tool accessible from PDP and checkout — resolves #1 pre-purchase blocker before users reach payment
Introduce mini/trial sizes at $10–$15 for IA's broad audience — reduces first-purchase risk, increases conversion from price-sensitive Super-Mom persona
Run cross-channel attribution analysis to quantify how much of the IC→Purchase gap is measurement artifact vs real behavioral drop-off

Strategic Analysis

01

IA wins on every metric except checkout completion

Across 22 days, IA Test beat BAU Control on CPM (−25%), CPA (−21%), ROAS (+35%), purchases (+27%), AOV (+6.5%), and click-to-ATC rate (+13%). The single exception is checkout-to-purchase rate, where Control holds a 6.5pp advantage — a solvable conversion problem, not a fundamental strategy flaw.

02

The lift test confirms mid-funnel incrementality

InitiateCheckout winning score of 87.7% is the clearest signal in the test. IA generates strongly incremental checkout intent. The weaker Purchase winning score (62.5%) is consistent with checkout drop-off suppressing the purchase signal — not a sign that IA fails to drive true demand.

03

Identical delivery patterns rule out media mix as a variable

Platform allocation (IG 57% / FB 43%), placement split (Feed 61% / Reels 26% / Stories 13%), and device mix (iPhone 83% / Android 10%) are nearly identical between both campaigns. The performance gap is driven by audience quality and bid strategy, not by where ads are running.

04

Recommendation: scale IA with checkout fixes

IA's lower CPM and higher reach efficiency make it the superior prospecting strategy. The checkout gap is addressable through stronger trust signals (explicit returns language + review count) and shade confidence tools. Fixing checkout conversion for IA's broader audience could yield 69+ incremental purchases per test period at $89 AOV.
