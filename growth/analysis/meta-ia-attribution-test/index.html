Strategic Interpretation
What the data is telling us
Media Buying: IA Accesses Cheaper Inventory

The IA Test strategy produces a ~24% lower CPM ($31.11 vs $40.87). For the same $81K spend, it delivered 620K more impressions and 570K more reach. This suggests the incremental attribution bidding model is targeting different — likely broader or less auction-competitive — inventory pools that BAU’s standard bidding avoids.

The CPM Tradeoff: Reach vs. Click Intent

Lower CPM correlates with lower CTR (1.32% vs 1.54%). The IA strategy is optimizing for reach efficiency and incremental exposure rather than high-intent clicks. This is expected — cheaper placements tend to generate less active engagement. The question is whether broader reach still drives real purchase lift, which is what the Conversion Lift test is designed to answer.

Conversion Lift: Both Beat Baseline, Test vs. Control Inconclusive

Both Cell 2 (BAU Control, $138) and Cell 3 (IA Test, $130) significantly outperform Cell 1 (All BAU, -$795 — negative incremental). However, the IA Test’s advantage over BAU Control is only a 62.5% winning score — well below the 80%+ threshold for meaningful confidence. The test ends April 7; final read should wait until then.

New Customer Nuance: Cell 1 Leads Here

Cell 1 (All BAU campaigns combined) shows the lowest new customer CPA at $117, beating both the IA Test ($135) and BAU Control ($150) at 94.9–97.3% confidence. This may reflect a composition effect — Cell 1 includes all prospecting activity, not just the two test cells. Interpret with caution, but it suggests IA Test hasn’t definitively improved new customer acquisition yet.

InitiateCheckout: IA Test’s Strongest Signal

The clearest win for the IA strategy: $95.54 vs $115 cost per incremental checkout, with an 87.7% winning score against BAU Control. This is above the informal significance threshold and suggests the IA strategy is driving more genuine mid-funnel engagement — people who intent to buy, not just browse.

Upper Funnel: BAU Wins AddToCart and ViewContent

Cell 1 (All BAU) dominates AddToCart ($8.77, 100% winning score) and ViewContent ($2.79, 100% winning score) vs both test cells. This is consistent with BAU’s higher CTR — it’s driving more active on-site engagement. The IA strategy sacrifices some of this upper-funnel efficiency in exchange for broader, cheaper reach. Whether the tradeoff is net-positive requires the full test readout.

Bottom Line — April 3 Read (Test Ongoing)

The IA Test strategy is clearly more media-efficient: it buys ~24% cheaper CPM, reaches 32% more unique users, and drives 26% more Meta-attributed purchases at a 21% lower CPA. The Conversion Lift data shows both test cells significantly outperform the baseline “All BAU” scenario. However, the direct IA vs. BAU Control comparison on incremental purchases is not yet conclusive (62.5% winning score). The strongest positive signal is the InitiateCheckout metric at 87.7%. The final read should happen after April 7 when the test officially ends — at which point a full confidence interval can be established. If the pattern holds, expect to recommend shifting budget toward the IA strategy.

Data Notes & Methodology
  • Attribution vs. Incrementality Meta-reported ROAS, purchases, and revenue figures (0.88× / 795 purchases for IA Test) are based on Meta’s standard attribution model, not the Conversion Lift holdout study. These numbers overstate true incrementality and should not be used to judge the test outcome. The Conversion Lift results are the statistically rigorous measure.
  • Placement & Age Breakdown Unavailable Placement-level data (Facebook Feed, Instagram Feed, Reels, Audience Network, Stories) and age/demographic breakdowns were not available via the API used for this analysis. A more granular audit may reveal whether the CPM difference is driven by placement mix shifts — e.g., IA Test serving more Audience Network or Reels vs. BAU.
  • Conversion Lift Cells Cell 1 is the holdout for all BAU campaigns combined — not just the two test campaigns. This makes direct Cell 1 comparisons directionally useful but not a clean head-to-head. The most meaningful comparison for this test is Cell 2 vs. Cell 3 (BAU Control vs. IA Test).
  • Test Window Data covers March 16 – April 2 (18 of 22 days). The remaining ~4 days (April 3–7) may shift the conversion lift confidence intervals, particularly for lower-volume objectives like Purchase. Winning scores below 80% should be treated as directional only until test end.
