Underpowered Test Flags

This test triggers multiple underpowered flags: (1) ran only 6 days — below the 7-day minimum to avoid weekday/weekend confounders; (2) lift sizes of ~1–3% require longer runtime to reach significance at the observed traffic volumes; (3) no active variant exceeded p2bc 0.20. The test should have been designed to run at least 14 days, or focused traffic allocation on fewer variants.

Section 08

Excluded Variants

Split — 0% Traffic Allocation (Excluded)

The "Split" variant was paused at some point during the test, resulting in 0% live traffic allocation. However, it still accumulated ~172,462 visitors and 4,372 orders — this is historical data from before it was paused. Its apparent performance (+5.47% NRPV, p2bc 77.98%) cannot be treated as a fair comparison to the 6-day test window of the other variants. The higher p2bc is likely inflated by different time-period composition (possibly a period with higher baseline conversion). All Split data is excluded from winner determination and follow-on recommendations.

Section 09

Key Learnings

  • Shorter hero copy is the best active challenger on mobile NRPV +3.29% on mobile with p2bc 89% — the strongest directional signal from any active variant on the dominant device. Shorter copy may reduce friction for mobile users who process less text before scrolling.
  • The NRPV lift comes from AOV, not CVR Shorter's +2.93% sitewide NRPV is driven primarily by +$1.35 AOV ($86.43 vs $85.08), not CVR (+0.03pp). This suggests copy brevity filters for higher-intent users rather than adding volume — a basket-quality effect, not a traffic-quality effect.
  • Social proof adds mobile value but hurts desktop Social Proof + Shorter is +2.57% NRPV on mobile (p2bc 83%) but -0.24% on desktop (p2bc 48%). Desktop converts at 3x the mobile rate, making the desktop regression significant enough to rule Social Proof + Shorter out as a universal winner.
  • 6 days is too short — test needs at least 14 days for these lift sizes With ~353K visitors per variant over 6 days, lifts of 1–3% will not reach 95% p2bc without a much longer runtime. The test was correctly designed for traffic split but underpowered for time. Future hero tests should be pre-planned for a minimum of 14 days or powered with sample size calculations upfront.
Section 10

Follow-On Test Hypotheses

01

Shorter vs Control — 14-Day Retest

Run a clean 2-way test (Shorter vs Control only, 50/50 split) for a minimum of 14 days. The directional signal is strong enough on mobile to warrant a conclusive test. Removes multi-variant dilution and gives full statistical power.

Primary metric: NRPV / Secondary: mobile CVR
02

Shorter + Mobile-Specific Hero Image

Shorter copy drives more Just Enough TM exploration per the journey data. Test a hero image featuring the TM or a skin-finish product alongside Shorter copy — hypothesis: copy + visual alignment amplifies the basket-widening effect seen in this test.

Primary metric: AOV + Blushing Stick revenue/visitor
03

Editorial on Desktop Only (Geotargeted)

Editorial was the desktop winner (+4.11% NRPV, p2bc 85.6%) but dragged down by mobile underperformance. Serve Editorial to desktop users only (via Intelligems device targeting) while Shorter runs on mobile — testing the device-specific creative hypothesis directly.

Primary metric: desktop NRPV / mobile NRPV
