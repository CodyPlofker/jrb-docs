Executive Summary

The quiz loses more people than it converts

56% of visitors who see the quiz intro never answer a single question. Of the 155K sessions this month, roughly 75K people bounced at the starting line. The quiz converts 30% lower per session than simply sending users to the Miracle Balm PDP.

Customer intelligence from 28 studies confirms the root causes: shade uncertainty is the #1 friction point site-wide (10+ source confirmations), yet the quiz's undertone step actively makes it worse. The primary customer persona (60+, 28.6% of revenue) encounters font sizes noted as "too small" and an 18-step gauntlet designed for a younger, more patient user.

Critical

56% never start the quiz

75,000 people see the intro and leave. Five compounding causes identified via live page audit.

  • A
    "01 / 18" step counter visible on every screen Tells users upfront this is an 18-step commitment. The intro copy says "a few quick questions" — 18 steps is not quick. 5 of those 18 are interstitials (testimonials, education, review confirmation) inflating the count. Removing non-question steps would show ~12 steps. Hiding the counter entirely removes the anxiety altogether.
  • B
    6-second page load, 22MB payload, 70 scripts, 610 resources On real mobile networks (3G/4G), this could easily be 15–20 seconds. Many users bounce before the quiz even renders. A quiz page should be lightweight — it's fundamentally a form.
  • C
    JavaScript error on every page load TypeError: Cannot read properties of null (reading 'includes') from a MutationObserver at line 460. May cause rendering failures for some users, especially on older mobile browsers (relevant given 67.3 avg customer age).
  • D
    CTA button barely visible on mobile On iPhone-sized viewports (390px), the "FIND MY SHADE" button sits at the bottom edge. Users may not realize there's an action to take. The intro hero image pushes the CTA below the natural scanning zone.
  • E
    Weak value proposition "Answer a few quick questions to help us find your miracle balm shade." No urgency, no benefit framing, no social proof. Compare to: "50,000+ perfect matches found. Get yours in 2 minutes." The copy also contradicts the visible "01/18" counter.
