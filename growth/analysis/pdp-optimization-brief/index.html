Evidence Deep Dives

Top 5 Fixes — Full Evidence

1
Shade Confidence Layer
Shade PDPs: Lip Recharge, WTF, JETM, Face Pencil, Eyeshadow Stick
46% prospect friction
7,048 CX tickets/qtr
50.5% score 1-3 shade confidence
78% want multi-skin-tone swatches
10 research sources confirm

The Problem

The current shade selector on JRB PDPs shows a swatch dot and a name. There is no visualization of what the color looks like on actual skin. The Lip Recharge heatmap shows 141K–83K shade clicks across 8 shades compared to just 40K ATC clicks. People are toggling between shades trying to decide. They never get the confidence to commit.

The modal being dismissed 66,057 times is significant — it suggests whatever pops up after clicking a shade is not resolving the uncertainty. They look, then close, then look again.

"The shade matching part was confusing — I wasn't sure which undertone I am." — ListenLabs prospect, PDP study
"I feel like with makeup, especially when you're buying it online, it's really helpful to have a before and after picture..." — ListenLabs prospect

Heatmap Signal

Lip Recharge: top 8 shade clicks = 850,657 total interactions. ATC = 40,040. That's a 21:1 ratio of shade exploration to purchase. Modal close = 66K (people escape without buying).

Survey Data

50.5% score 1-3 on shade confidence (Website Feedback, n=91). WTF Quiz: avg 6.6/10 confidence, 28.3% low confidence. MB Quiz: avg 7.3/10 — even lower for new customers.

CX Volume

7,048 shade/undertone tickets per quarter = 24% of all CX volume. This is the single largest driver of customer service cost. Fixing it on-page reduces support load and improves conversion simultaneously.

Competitive Benchmark

Ilia Beauty deploys multiple dedicated shade finders per product (Serum Foundation, Skin Tint, Concealer, Complexion Stick — each with own tool). Fenty Beauty uses AR "try this shade" directly on the PDP. Both brands have significantly lower shade friction.

Recommendation

Add a "Shade Preview" component directly in the shade selector row. When a shade is selected, show: (1) a larger swatch rendered on 3 skin tone swatches (light/medium/deep), (2) a 1-2 line shade description written in benefit language (not just "warm peachy nude"), (3) a "best for [undertone]" tag. No popup, no modal, no email gate. Inline. This keeps the customer in the buy flow rather than throwing them into a detour.

For Lip Recharge specifically: add "as worn" lifestyle image per shade when possible — even a single model shot applying the shade does more than any copy description.

2
Reviews Above the Fold
All PDPs
Reviews below fold = conversion killer
JRB — "Glaring Zero problem"
240K+ Junip reviews available

The Problem

On most JRB PDPs the star rating and review count appear below the gallery and description. By the time a customer reaches reviews, they've already formed a positive or negative impression without the strongest social proof. Additionally, any PDP that has zero reviews shows a glaring blank — which actively destroys trust.

"I want to see reviews before I scroll down." — ListenLabs prospect (unprompted, multiple studies)
"I trust Ulta that has a lot of different brands more than just a main brand." — ListenLabs prospect, on brand-site reviews

Direct Customer Ask

Customers explicitly requested reviews visible before they scroll. This is not inferred behavior — it was stated outright across multiple ListenLabs interview studies.

Competitive Benchmark

Rare Beauty, Merit, and Ilia all display star rating + review count directly below the product name in the above-fold buybox. This is the industry standard for DTC beauty PDPs that convert.

Recommendation

Move the star rating widget (stars + count) to immediately below the product name, above price. This is a CSS/template change — the data is already there. Additionally, ensure no shade or variant with zero reviews inherits the parent product rating rather than showing zero. Hide the review widget on variants with no reviews rather than showing "0 reviews."

3
Sticky Mobile ATC Bar
All PDPs — Mobile Priority
63%+ traffic is mobile
Design system rule: ATC visible without scroll
850K shade interactions vs 40K ATC

The Problem

On mobile, the ATC button lives in the standard buybox. Once a shopper scrolls down through the gallery or into the description, the ATC disappears. Given that the heatmap data shows people are doing extensive shade browsing — scrolling through gallery images, re-clicking shades, reading descriptions — the ATC button is chronically off-screen during the highest-intent moments.

The JRB design system explicitly states: "Add to Cart must be visible without scrolling on mobile." This rule is being violated during the decision phase.

Traffic Reality

63%+ of JRB traffic is mobile. WTF avg time on page = 122 seconds — shoppers are engaged and scrolling. Every second the ATC is off-screen is a missed conversion moment.

Competitive Standard

Rhode Skin, Ilia, and Fenty all use sticky ATC bars on mobile that appear once the user scrolls past the initial buybox. Often includes selected shade name to confirm the choice before adding.

Recommendation

Add a sticky bottom bar on mobile that appears after the user scrolls past the buybox. Bar should contain: selected shade name (or "Select a shade" if none selected), price, and "Add to Cart" button. Disappears when back at top. This is a well-established pattern with strong industry evidence and directly addresses the shade-browsing scroll problem revealed in the heatmap.

4
Value Proof Block Above ATC
All PDPs
71% price/value friction
26.4% of MB buyers almost didn't buy — price
22% want return policy clarity

The Problem

JRB products are priced at a premium ($28–$85). Price/value concerns are the #1 friction point by volume (71% of prospects). The current PDP shows price and an ATC button with little surrounding context to justify the spend. There is no persistent trust reinforcement near the buy decision.

Customers specifically want return policy specifics — "free and easy returns" is too vague. They want to know the time window, whether opened products qualify, and whether there's a money-back guarantee.

"With makeup especially online, I want to know I can return it if the shade is wrong." — ListenLabs prospect, PDP study (paraphrase of recurring theme)

Price Anxiety at Purchase

26.4% of actual Miracle Balm buyers (n=4,876) said they almost didn't purchase due to price. That's 1 in 4 buyers who had to overcome a price objection. Surfacing value cues near ATC would reduce this friction before it becomes hesitation.

What to Include

Free shipping threshold, specific return window (e.g., "30-day return on any product"), clean beauty badge (no parabens/sulfates), and if applicable, Klarna/Afterpay availability. Icons + short text. No walls of copy.

Recommendation

Add a 3-icon trust strip directly below the ATC button. Example: Free Shipping on $50+ | 30-Day Returns | Clean Beauty Certified. Keep it scannable — icon + 2-4 words each. This is a copy and template change (Ease: 7). Note: JRB27 (returns copy below ATC) washed — but that was text-only copy. A visual trust strip with specific policy details is a different approach.

5
Age-Inclusive PDP Imagery
All PDPs
46% of prospects flagged age gap
16.4% of buyers explicitly requested
Core customer avg age: 60+ (32% of revenue)

The Problem

The 60+ Ageless Matriarch persona is 28.6% of customers and 32.2% of revenue — the single most valuable segment. Yet product gallery imagery skews visually young. This creates cognitive dissonance for the actual buyer: the person in the photo doesn't look like her, so the product feels "not for me."

"The website seems geared toward 20 to 30 year olds." — Actual JRB customer, Website Feedback survey

Who Actually Buys

65+ = 65.6% of the Ageless Matriarch segment, which is 32.2% of revenue. The Healthcare Practitioner (11.6% of customers) avg age is 50. The High-Powered Executive is 48.6. The actual buyer skews 45-68. The gallery should reflect this.

Ease Rating: 8

This is primarily an image swap — existing Air library has real JRB photography. No code changes required. Highest ease of any meaningful fix on this list. The constraint is sourcing/selecting age-appropriate models from existing library.

Recommendation

Audit PDP hero gallery images across top 5 PDPs. For each PDP, ensure at least 2 of the gallery slots show women in the 40-65+ range. This is an image swap (Ease: 8). Start with Miracle Balm and WTF as highest-revenue PDPs. This also helps with Lip Recharge — showing the product on an age-appropriate model reduces the "is this for me?" gap for the core buyer.

What 10 Top Brands Do on Their PDPs

Audited April 14, 2026. 10 brands visited live via Chrome MCP (6 beauty + 4 best-in-class non-beauty). Each PDP was opened, text-scraped, and screenshot-captured. This section maps what's actually on the internet today against JRB's known friction points.

Universal Patterns — Table Stakes (7+ brands)

Baseline expectations. JRB already does most of these; each should be gut-checked against the competitive bar.

Star rating + review count above the fold

Merit 6,911 · ILIA 15,144 · Kosas 2,192 · Rare 4,876 · Westman 1,318 · Rhode 3,942. Review count itself is a social-proof signal.

Gallery mixes product + swatches + on-model

Every beauty PDP includes model shots (diverse skin tones), swatch flat-lays, and packaging shots in the main carousel.

Accordion details (Ingredients, How to Use, Details)

Saie, Westman, Merit, Rhode all collapse long-form info into accordions to keep the above-fold clean.

One-Time vs Subscribe & Save toggle

Saie (with "Every 1 month" frequency dropdown), ILIA, Westman all offer ~10% subscribe discount. JRB doesn't.

Installment financing inline with price

Afterpay (Rhode), Shop Pay (Away), PayPal Pay in 4 (Saie). Usually shown directly under the price — not in footer.

Free shipping / easy returns near ATC

Allbirds, Westman, Merit, Away, Rhode all reassure policy right next to or below the ATC button.

Shade Finder / Find My Shade in main nav

Merit, Kosas, ILIA, Rare all have "Find My Shade" / "Shade Finder" as a top-nav link, not buried.

Clean / certification badges

Clean, Vegan, Cruelty-Free, Dermatologist Tested, Hypoallergenic, Non-Comedogenic — on Kosas, Rare, Merit, ILIA, Westman.

Common Patterns — Worth Adopting (4–6 brands)

Press awards overlaid on hero imagery

ILIA stacks 3 award badges (1% for the Planet, Allure 2020, Byrdie Eco Beauty). Saie shows Allure Best of Beauty. Merit places Allure sticker on gallery. Visible at zero scroll.

Shade grouping by family with labeled tiers

Merit groups by mood ("The Pops," "The Neutrals," "The Classic Pinks"). ILIA + Kosas let users filter by depth tab + undertone. Live descriptor like "Extra light with neutral undertones."

Cross-sell as "Complete the Routine" / "Pair With"

Westman ("Pair With: Foundation Brush + Concealer"), Rhode ("Complete the Routine" numbered 01–05), Rare ("You May Also Like"). Mini-bundle framing beats generic recs.

On-model thumbnails inside the main gallery

Merit, Kosas, Rare embed diverse-skin-tone model shots in the PDP carousel — not a separate section. Makes shade decisions faster.

UGC / creator section on PDP

Rare Beauty surfaces 8+ creator handles in a dedicated section. Saie and Merit use award callouts similarly.

Clinical / efficacy claims block

Kosas: "100% agreed Revealer Concealer improved the quality of bare skin" (4× stat block). Westman references skin-calming actives.

Differentiators — Unique Approaches Worth Testing (1–3 brands)

  • Kosas BEFORE / AFTER imagery with clinical proof. Direct before/after photos paired with "100% agreed" percentages. Best-in-class for efficacy-anxious shoppers. Exact match to JRB customer asks.
  • Away "COMPARE CARRY-ONS" + "AIRLINE COMPATIBILITY" secondary CTAs. Decision tools placed next to the product title. JRB could test "Compare Shades" or "Which Miracle Balm is right for me?"
  • Away 360° product view + editor-award overlay. "Our Pick by Gear Patrol" badge on the first image is clean authority.
  • Westman Atelier "Book a complimentary video session: clean beauty concierge." White-glove 1:1 consultation as a top-of-page link. Premium service as differentiator.
  • Rhode full-bleed auto-playing editorial video hero. No text overlay, just close-up beauty video. More editorial than retail.
  • Rhode component-level packaging sustainability breakdown. "Cap: PP #5, 75% PCR. Bottle + Pump: 35% PCR." Specific, not hand-wavy.
  • ILIA live shade descriptor text updates as you hover/click swatches ("Extra light with neutral undertones / SKYE ST.5"). Paired with "Find My Shade" + "ILIA Shade Guide" links right beneath the swatch grid.
  • Apple monthly-financing math and trade-in credit. "From $1,099 or $45.79/mo" done for the customer; trade-in anchors the "real" price far below sticker.
  • Allbirds one-line proof stack: "$110 + FREE SHIPPING (6690)" — price, policy, and social proof in a single line above the size picker.
  • Rare Beauty "1% of sales supports the Rare Impact Fund" — mission-driven purchase motivation baked into the ATC area.

Gaps / Whitespace — What Nobody Does That JRB Could Own

Customer-contributed before/after gallery

Kosas shows one marketing before/after; nobody has a customer-contributed gallery. JRB's audience has explicitly asked for this. Highest-leverage whitespace.

Shade click → real customer try-on gallery

Nobody shows 3–5 real customer selfies in the selected shade under the picker. Most-direct fix for the 46% shade-anxiety friction.

Explicit value / price-per-use breakdown

No competitor reframes price against usage ("$32 / 60 uses = $0.53 per application"). Given JRB's 71% price/value concern, this is untouched.

Sticky mobile ATC with live shade preview

Westman has a floating ATC but doesn't show the selected shade swatch in the sticky bar. Small upgrade nobody owns.

Honest "Who this is NOT for" callout

All brands over-promise ("for everyone"). A confident JRB-voice statement on skin type, coverage level, or use case could build trust.

AI shade-match via phone camera

Not present on any PDP audited. Emerging tech; would be bold and on-brand for a shade-picky audience.

What NOT to Copy

  • Over-claiming with "100% agreed" — Works for Kosas because they ran a clinical study. Don't fake it.
  • Rhode's sparse-text approach — Leans on the founder halo (Hailey Bieber). JRB is a founder brand too, but the audience needs more proof density, not less.
  • Allbirds' no-model approach — JRB is a color-cosmetics brand; on-model is mandatory for shade confidence.
Optimal PDP Vision

What the Ideal JRB PDP Looks Like

Section-by-section description of the optimal PDP with all fixes applied. This is the V4 target — the version that would score highest if all improvements are implemented. Variations strip components back from here.

Zone 1
Above Fold — The Buybox
  • Product name in Canela Light (headline treatment)
  • Star rating + review count immediately below name — clickable, anchors to reviews section

  • Shade selector with visual swatch dots — when a shade is selected, a "shade preview" inline block appears showing the swatch on 3 skin tones (light/medium/deep) plus a 1-line benefit description

Gallery — Proof Imagery
  • First 1-2 gallery images: product beauty shot (existing)
  • 3rd+ gallery images: "as worn" lifestyle shots on women 40-65+ — represents actual buyer, not aspirational 25-year-old
  • If product has shades: at least 1 gallery image shows color applied on skin (before/after or "as worn" per shade) — even a single hero shade demo image improves shade confidence
  • Gallery clickable to full-screen zoom — currently underutilized (40K gallery clicks on Lip Recharge alone)
Zone 3
Product Description — The Convince
  • JRB06 copy already won and is deployed — maintain the winning format (clear structure, bold focus words, simplified language)
  • Key ingredients callout: 3-4 hero ingredients with 1-line benefit each (not full INCI list). Responds to 54% ingredient transparency friction and Healthcare Practitioner persona.
  • How-to-use: 2-3 steps, simple and scannable. Reduces post-purchase complaints about application.
Zone 4
Shade Finder Inline CTA
  • On shade-heavy PDPs (WTF, Lip Recharge, JETM, Face Pencil): a secondary CTA "Not sure which shade?" links to a non-gated, product-specific shade recommendation flow
  • No popup, no email gate — this was the failure mode of JRB24 and JRB28. Inline link to a quiz page (or inline quiz widget)
  • Returns a recommended shade with a clickable "select this shade" back to the PDP
Zone 5
Social Proof — Reviews Section
  • Junip reviews section already exists — keep it, but move the star summary widget above fold
  • Add shade filter to review display — "show reviews for [Petal]" — highest-value enhancement to reviews section for shade products
  • Feature 2-3 pinned "best" reviews at top before sort — curated, not just most-recent
  • Never show a product with 0 reviews without redirecting the count to parent product
Zone 6
Press + Trust Credentials
  • JRB08 (Vogue Beauty Award) is currently testing — results pending
  • If JRB08 wins: press credential lives near ATC or in the trust strip below ATC
  • Do not add a full press logo bar section (JRB19 ended unclear) — a single badge/seal near ATC is lower friction and more focused
