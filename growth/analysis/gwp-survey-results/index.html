What We Heard — Key Themes

1
"Let us try new products first"

101 votes Top Pick

New or unreleased products was the #1 product GWP choice by a wide margin. Customers want the insider feeling of being first to try. This creates a natural launch amplifier — GWP as product seeding.

2
Tools and brushes are under-leveraged

87 votes High Demand

Tools/accessories came in 3rd in the structured question, but dominated open-ended responses. "Mini brushes!" "Travel brush set!" "The Everything Brush with MB purchase!" appeared repeatedly. Customers see JRB tools as aspirational but under-sampled.

3
Tiered GWPs drive higher spend

64.4% score 4-5 Revenue lever

Customers explicitly say tiered thresholds motivate them to add to cart. One respondent: "I actually added more to my cart than I was going to initially get because I wanted the 2nd GWP." The Clinique and Estee Lauder tiered model was cited multiple times as aspirational.

4
Skip gifts from other brands

Avg 2.53/5 Avoid

The data is clear: customers want JRB products, not gifts from partner brands. "I stay loyal to JRB" and "No, only own brand" were common refrains. The Cadence collab had defenders, but was the exception. If a collab happens, it needs to feel like an extension of JRB, not a cross-promo.

5
Holiday kits are the unlock

107 holiday responses High engagement

The holiday question generated the most open-ended responses. Customers want themed, curated kits: "spa set for Mother's Day," "sparkle for July 4th," "new fall colors for Labor Day." The word "kits" appeared in nearly a third of responses. They also want kits designed for gifting, not just self-purchase.

On execution details

Bonus: Recurring Asks Worth Noting

Loyalty / rewards program came up unprompted in multiple responses. Customers want to feel recognized for repeat purchases. Not in scope for GWP planning, but a clear signal for the retention team.
Fragrance samples were requested frequently — customers want to try the perfume before committing to full-size. Mini rollerballs or scent strips would be high-value, low-cost GWPs.
Choose-your-own options came up repeatedly. Customers want shade choice or product choice rather than a one-size GWP. "I have received gifts with purchase where I could choose the color, which I really appreciate because a gift is only good if I will actually use it."

Recommended Actions

Now

Lead with new product GWPs

Use upcoming launches as GWP moments. Seed new products as minis with purchase to create trial and buzz. Customers want to feel like insiders.

Now

Implement tiered thresholds

Spend $75 get X, spend $125 get Y. 64% of customers say this motivates higher spend. The data backs this up clearly.

Next

Build holiday kit calendar

Map themed kits to key holidays: Mother's Day (pampering/gifting), July 4th (summer glow), Labor Day (fall transition). Curate, don't just discount.

Next

Tools as GWP test

Mini Everything Brush or travel brush set with $100+ purchase. High perceived value, high demand signal from this survey.

Next

Add shade/product choice

When possible, let customers pick their GWP shade or product. Increases perceived value and reduces waste.

Explore

Fragrance minis

Perfume rollerballs or sample sprays as low-cost, high-interest GWPs. Reduces the barrier to try a $90+ fragrance.
