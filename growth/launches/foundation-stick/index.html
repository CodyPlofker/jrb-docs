This document is the canonical creative strategy for the Foundation Stick launch. It synthesizes the strategic brief, launch brief, customer intelligence, competitive landscape, and the JRB creative strategy system (playbook, archetypes, format library, copy frameworks) into one operational plan. The downstream /static-ads Toolkit run plan in §9 generates 200+ statics across this strategy.

§1Executive Summary

The Strategic Territory

The first foundation stick that foundation-stick haters will love.

The Audience

Foundation skeptics — women who’ve written off the format because they’ve been burned by every stick they’ve tried. Primary personas: High-Powered Executive (premium buyer, 30s-50s, $114 AOV) and Suburban Super-Mom (highest growth potential, $112 AOV, retention-fragile). Secondary: Wellness Practitioner and Creative Entrepreneur. Both primaries hit at Exploration phase (active comparing) and Problem-Aware through Product-Aware awareness.

The Differentiator

Real coverage AND weightless feel. Every other stick trades one for the other. We have the claim data to defeat Merit on their own turf:

  • 100% felt comfortable on skin
  • 97% felt hydrating
  • 94% didn’t feel any product weight
  • 92% felt unlike other stick foundations
  • +51% hydration increase at 30 minutes
  • 8-hour wear without oxidizing or settling

The Campaign Concept

Calling All Foundation Stick Haters — a stance that turns the category’s biggest skeptics into the brand’s biggest evangelists. Every concept ladders to this stance + the proof.

The 12 Category Complaints We Name and Defeat

Cakey · Heavy · Streaky · Separates · Thick · Creasing · Powdery · Chalky · Greasy · Patchy · Oxidizing · Settling

The Creative Output

~200 static ads across 8 angles × 4 personas × 6 formats, generated via 8 sequential /static-ads Toolkit runs (see §9). Plus video, advertorial, partnership, and Bobbi-led content tracked separately by builder skills.

The Feedback Hooks

This launch is the test of the JRB creative strategy system end-to-end — strategy → format library → copy frameworks → static-ads engine. §11 catalogs what to retrospect after the 5/29 2-week recap.

§2Strategic Foundation

2.1 — Product Positioning (from launch brief §08)

Positioning Statement

For the woman who wants coverage that never feels like a compromise, Your Skin Foundation Stick is the first stick foundation with a skincare-grade formula — delivering buildable, natural coverage that feels as good at hour eight as it does at hour one.

Positioning levers we own

  • Skin-first as a format, not a claim — Merit owns “Minimalist,” JRB owns “skin-first as a format.” Subtle but defensible.
  • Buildable AND lightweight as the same idea — every other stick treats them as a tradeoff.
  • Skincare-grade formula (Ceramide NP + HA + squalane + shea butter) — we have the ingredient story Merit doesn’t lead with.

30 shades — Pale Alabaster → Espresso. Up from 18 (per launch brief §09). Bigger than Merit’s range and on par with Fenty.

Critical: shade match is the #1 unmet customer need (PENTA-CONFIRMED in customer-intelligence — 46% prospect mention, 32% PDP study, 24% of 7,048 quarterly CX tickets, 26.9% of 1,185 non-purchasers cite as barrier).

Creative implication: The shade-range hero must be a primary Wave 1 format. Both lineup (vertical) and grid (matrix) versions per the Merit Uniform shade lineup + Merit 30-stick grid references in INSPO.md.

Per the strategic brief and INSPO.md: Merit is the explicit direct competitor. The launch creative succeeds or fails on whether it visually and verbally distinguishes from Merit’s $38 Minimalist Complexion Stick.

4.3 — Visual Differentiators (Must Be in Every Static)

  • JRB packaging design (white/cream tube + black wordmark, NOT Merit’s dark-brown + gold)
  • Used / lived-in product aesthetic — show the swipe, the finger mark, the in-use moment
  • Cream/white grounds (NEVER Merit’s dark-brown surfaces or saturated colors)
  • Canela italic typography (overlaps with Merit’s italic serif but distinguishable)
  • Real-skin imagery with imperfections visible in BEFORE shots (Westman Atelier-style honesty)

§5The Creative Strategy

5.1 — The Diagnosis (1-line)

Diagnosis

Foundation Stick must defeat the 12-complaint category prejudice — customers want stick-convenience but disbelieve any ‘lightweight’ claim. We lead with disagreeable stance (the haters) + visceral mechanism + clinical proof, in a Stage 4-5 saturated category where claim-alone won’t move anyone, while differentiating from Merit on stance + voice (skincare-grade, hater-validated) and visual signature (lived-in, not gallery).

§8Funnel Placement & Ad Account Structure

8.1 — Cold-Traffic TOF (broad)

  • Angles: A1 (Convert), A2 (12 Complaints), A7 (Skin-First Reframe)
  • Spend: 60-70% of paid spend
  • Persona signal-load: Exec + Mom (broadest)

8.2 — Warm/MOF (interest + retargeting)

  • Angles: A3 (Clinical-Stat), A4 (Mechanism), A6 (Stress Test)
  • Spend: 25-30%
  • Trigger: PDP visit, JETM purchase history, Mini MB customer

8.3 — BOF / Retargeting

  • Angles: A5 (Side-by-Side), A8 (Demand-Validation), shade-range static (W1.14)
  • Spend: 5-10%
  • Trigger: PDP visit + cart-add, abandoned shade-quiz

8.4 — Per-Ad-Set per-Concept Structure

  • Each Wave 1 angle = its own ad set
  • Static + 9:16 variants of the same concept run together
  • Variations within ad set vary: hook, headline, persona-imagery
  • Meta auto-allocates to winners
