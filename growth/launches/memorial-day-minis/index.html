§1Executive Summary

What this is & why we're doing it

A 7-day Memorial Day promotion across the JRB minis assortment — up to 30% off plus a GWP at the right basket threshold. We're running it because Memorial Day is a high-intent shopping moment, minis are an accelerating consumer trend, and the format gives curious-but-uncommitted shoppers the lowest-friction way to try the staples and trade up to full size.

The diagnosis. Memorial Day Mini Sale needs to convert latent curiosity into low-commitment discovery sessions, leveraging proven demand for minis — without letting price-led creative compound the wrong value perception.

The offer is one lever, not the whole pitch. The 7-day window has multiple value props the creative should rotate through, and each persona responds to a different one:

  • Up to 30% off. The price unlock. Real, but used as the reason to act now — not the headline.
  • The free gift. A GWP at the right basket threshold reinforces the trade-up thesis. Mechanic locked by May 14.
  • First chance to try the minis. Limited-edition format. People have been eyeing the staples; this is the lowest-commitment way in.
  • Travel-friendly. Memorial Day is a travel weekend. The carry-on, hotel-bathroom, one-quart-bag angle owns the Executive and Supermom.
  • Summer-ready. School ends, bags change, routines reset. Owns the Supermom and Educator.
  • The "I've been wanting to try Jones Road" entry point. For the curious-but-hasn't-bought audience — mini removes the commitment friction. Owns the Matriarch and Wellness Practitioner.

Lead with whichever lever the persona responds to. Let the discount be the unlock that makes acting now obvious, but build the value perception across all six.

Three things make this campaign different from a normal launch:

  • No new product to position. The "product" is the permission to try plus the build-your-own kit mechanic. The creative job is to convert latent demand into discovery sessions.
  • The catalog is the assortment. Hero is not a single SKU — it's the curated set of staples: MMB, WTF, JETM, Eye Cream, Hippie Stick, brushes. Every persona has a different starter kit they should leave with.
  • The window is short. Peak shopping is May 24–26. Brand-build creative needs to launch before that; DR creative needs to be loaded and ready to scale on hot signal by Friday May 22.

Anti-rules for the campaign:

  • No "mature skin" or age-coded framing. Speak to identity, not age.
  • Don't go Bobbi-dependent. She can be the curator voice on one or two assets, but the brand has to own the discovery framing without her.
  • Don't go single-hero. Stack creative across MMB, WTF, JETM, Eye Cream, and Hippie Stick. Let conversion data reveal which mini-to-full-size route has the highest leverage.

What we're optimizing: discovery sessions per dollar and full-size attach rate post-MDW. The campaign succeeds when Mini MB buyers come back for full-size MMB by July.

§3The 6-Persona Matrix

Per the playbook: persona is the LAST filter, not the first. But every persona deserves a first-class playbook entry for this campaign. Below: every JRB persona with the tension MDW activates, the angle that lands, the format that fits, and the copy direction.

How to use this section. When briefing any creator, copywriter, or static-ads run for MDW, pick the persona first, then read the row in this matrix to lock the tension → angle → format → copy direction. Don't generalize across personas — the same angle ("travel-friend") sounds completely different in the Matriarch's voice vs the Executive's.

§6Competitive Defeat (vs ILIA, Merit, Haus Labs)

The strategic brief identified three competitors with active mini strategies. Each does a different thing — we win by NOT doing what they do.

CompetitorTheir playTheir weaknessJRB defeat
ILIA "Meet the Minis" set — 4 bestsellers as a curated discovery bundle. Gift-coded, cleanly merchandised, single SKU. You buy what they put in the box. No agency. Same kit for everyone. Lead with "Pick your minis. Build your kit." Custom over prescribed. Our angle A3 Stacking Logic exists explicitly to defeat ILIA's set approach.
Merit Trio of minis, anchored to the hero product (a complexion stick or blush). Seasonal/gifting-coded at Sephora. The hero anchor limits assortment. Customer can't really discover — the trio is the trio. JRB's mini assortment spans skincare + complexion + tools + eye/lip. 17+ SKUs. The breadth IS the differentiator. Lead creative with the catalog spread (not a single trio).
Haus Labs Shade-match acquisition tool — minis as a foundation-purchase-friction solver. DTC-only. Single-product (foundation) focus. Mini is a means to a full-size foundation sale, nothing else. JRB's mini play is broader — discovery across product types. Position against Haus Labs implicitly: "Not just shade-match. Full-routine match."

The defeat isn't about calling competitors out by name — per playbook §strong-stances, we call out category defaults, not brands. The defeat is structural: our angle slate covers ground the competitors don't — custom kits, breadth across categories, founder curation.
