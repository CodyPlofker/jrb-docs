The Hero Attention Problem

ListenLabs eye-tracking shows only 17% of visitors notice the hero section. 63% of first-scan attention goes to featured products. The hero is the most expensive real estate on the site — and it's being ignored.

The Implication

The homepage serves multiple modes: restockers who need fast nav, browsers who want discovery, and researchers who want depth. The architecture must serve all three — fast paths to product AND rich content for exploration.

Nobody Uses the Quiz First

0% of respondents use the quiz as their first homepage action. The quiz is a tool customers use after they've decided to buy — not a discovery mechanism. The homepage should prioritize product discovery (bestsellers, new arrivals, categories) over quiz/shade tools.

New Arrivals is the Runaway Winner

The Age Perception Problem

Multiple 45+ prospects felt the homepage was "oriented towards people much younger than me, like maybe Gen Z." Tracksuit brand health data shows JRB's audience skews 65+ with "Mature" imagery. But the brand aspires to reach 35+. The homepage needs to thread this needle — aspirational but inclusive, not skewing too young or too old.

Recommended Section Architecture

10 homepage sections, ordered by customer priority and behavior data. Each has 1–4 design variations to review.

1

Hero

Rethink the hero — it only gets 17% attention. Options: product-led (featured bestseller), category entry (fastest path to products), Bobbi-led (founder authority), or minimal text with trust strip. The hero should drive action, not just brand mood.

📊 17% attention · 38.5% go straight to nav · 28.2% scroll to browse

4 variations →
2

New Arrivals

The #1 most-wanted homepage section. 51.3% ranked it first. 50% say it's MISSING. Must include product name, price, star ratings, and review count on every card — proof signals that ListenLabs says featured products need.

📊 51.3% ranked #1 · 50% say missing · 94.9% top-3

4 variations →
3

Bobbi's Picks / Content

43.6% say Bobbi content is missing from the homepage. Options: curated product picks with Bobbi quotes, video-forward tutorial section, editorial quote block, or "Bobbi's Tip" recurring card. 55% rate Bobbi importance 5/5.

📊 43.6% say missing · 55% rate Bobbi 5/5 · 51.9% go to Bobbi social for education

4

Shop by Concern / Skin Type

42.3% say this is missing. Options: visual concern cards (Glow, Hydration, Sensitive, Anti-Aging), "I want to..." interactive chooser, horizontal skin type strip, or image-backed concern cards.

📊 42.3% say missing (#3 gap)

4 variations →

Featured Products / Bestsellers

63% of first-scan attention goes to featured products. Current tiles lack proof signals — need visible ratings, review counts, and prices on every card. "Bestseller" tags on top performers.

"Where to Start" Guide

Quiz / Shade Finder Promo

20.5% want the quiz easier to find. But it should NOT be the primary homepage CTA (0% use it first). Place it mid-page as a secondary path — dark two-column with shade swatches.

Press / As Seen In
